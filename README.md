# water_quality_monitoring_system
It is a necessary IOT product in recent times to check the water quality.
Water quality is of critical importance to public health, agricultural production, and environmental stewardship. The increased levels of pollution and contaminants in water have necessitated the monitoring of water quality. The traditional methods of water quality assessment involve physical sampling and analysis of water samples. These methods are not only time-consuming and expensive but also not feasible for real-time applications. Keeping this in mind, this research aims to design and implement an Internet of Things (IoT)-based water quality monitoring system using a Total Dissolved Solids sensor and ESP8266 microcontroller, which will be connected to the Blynk mobile app. The main aim of this research is to design a cost-effective, efficient, and convenient system of water quality assessment in real time.
In this research, a Total Dissolved Solids sensor will be used to detect the concentration of dissolved solids in water, which is an important parameter in determining the purity of water and its conductivity. The higher the concentration of Total Dissolved Solids in water, the greater the chances of the presence of salts, minerals, and other contaminants in water. The ESP8266 NodeMCU module will be connected to the sensor to receive the data in analog form and then convert it into digital form to transmit it via a Wi-Fi network.
To improve the usability and accessibility of the system, the Blynk application is utilized as a graphical user interface that displays the real-time water parameters. The system allows the user to monitor the TDS level remotely and take the required actions if the water quality is not within the acceptable limits. The system provides real-time data, which improves the response time compared to the traditional system. This system is applicable in smart homes, fish farming, and agricultural fields.
